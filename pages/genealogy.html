⭐ Notable Ancestors

👴
Harold Elias Crews (1899–1978)
Born: Carroll County, Georgia
Harold was a farmer and carpenter who built the original Crews family homestead in 1924. He served in the U.S. Army during WWI and was known throughout the county for his woodworking skill. The old homestead still stands today.
WWI Veteran Homesteader
👵
Edna Crews née Stafford (1903–1985)
Born: Coweta County, Georgia
Edna was a schoolteacher for over 30 years and the unofficial family historian. She kept meticulous journals from 1920 onward, which form the backbone of our family records today. Her journals are preserved in the family archive.
Educator Family Historian
👴
Robert E. Crews (1928–2010)
Born: Newnan, Georgia
Robert served in the Korean War and later built a successful hardware business in town. He was the first in the family to attend college and instilled a deep love of education in his children. Grandfather Bob, as he was called, was the anchor of every family reunion.
Korean War Veteran Entrepreneur
👵
Margaret Crews née Bishop (1932–2018)
Born: LaGrange, Georgia
"Grandma Maggie" was the heart of the Crews family for decades. An accomplished baker and quilter, her recipes are catalogued in the family cookbook project. She hand-stitched a quilt for each grandchild and great-grandchild — 23 quilts in total.
Quilter & Baker

📜 Family History Timeline

  • 📍
    1871 — Elias Crews born in Carroll County, Georgia. Family traces roots to English settlers in colonial South Carolina.
  • ⚔️
    1918 — Harold Crews enlists in U.S. Army. Serves in France during the final year of WWI.
  • 💍
    1921 — Harold Crews marries Edna Stafford in a ceremony at First Baptist Church, Newnan, GA.
  • 🏠
    1924 — Harold builds the family homestead on Heard County farmland. The property remains in the family.
  • 👶
    1928 — Robert E. Crews born, the eldest of four children.
  • ⚔️
    1952 — Robert Crews serves in the Korean War, 7th Infantry Division.
  • 💍
    1955 — Robert marries Margaret Bishop. James D. Crews born the same year.
  • 🏪
    1962 — Robert opens "Crews Hardware & Supply" on Main Street — a community fixture for 40 years.
  • 🎓
    1977 — James Crews graduates from University of Georgia with a degree in Business Administration.
  • 💍
    1979 — James Crews marries Patricia Allen. Michael Crews born the following year.
  • 📖
    1985 — Edna Crews passes away. Her 65-year journal collection is donated to the family archive.
  • 🏅
    2002 — "Crews Hardware & Supply" closes after 40 years; Robert retires with a community celebration in his honor.
  • 🏡
    2015 — The family begins the first Annual Crews Family Reunion, held every August at the homestead.
  • 📚
    2024 — Family Genealogy Database project launched to digitize all historical records and photos.

📍 Geographic Origins

🌿
Carroll County, Georgia
Paternal Line · Est. 1820s
The Crews name appears in Carroll County land records as early as 1826. Elias Crews was born here in 1871.
🌾
Coweta County, Georgia
Maternal Line · Est. 1840s
The Stafford and Bishop families (maternal lines) were established in Coweta County, Georgia by the mid-1800s.
🏛️
South Carolina Roots
Colonial Origins · 1700s
Earliest traceable ancestors arrived in colonial South Carolina from England in the early 1700s before migrating west to Georgia.
