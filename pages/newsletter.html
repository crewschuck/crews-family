📰 The Crews Chronicle — Spring 2026

Published: April 15, 2026 · Editor: Jennifer Crews-Moore · Issue #23

Hello Crews Family! Spring has arrived and it's bringing plenty of good news with it. Welcome to Issue #23 of The Crews Chronicle. In this edition:

  • 🍼 Big News: New addition to the family coming this Fall!
  • 🍳 Cookbook Update: Grandma Maggie's recipe collection nears completion
  • 🏠 Homestead Progress: The cabin is looking better than ever
  • 🎉 Reunion 2026: Save the date and theme announcement
  • 🌟 Family Spotlight: Susan's art show in Atlanta
  • 📚 Book Club: Our spring reading list
  • 🎓 Milestones: Graduations and achievements

🍼 A New Crew is on the Way!

Submitted by: David & Rachel Crews

We are overjoyed to share that David and Rachel Crews are expecting their first child this October! Baby Crews is due October 14th, and they've chosen to keep the gender a surprise until birth.

"We can't wait to introduce the newest member of the family," David shared. "We already know Grandpa James is going to spoil them absolutely rotten, and honestly we're okay with that." A family shower is being planned for late August — watch for the invite!

🍼 Exciting News!

🍳 Cookbook Nearly Complete!

Submitted by: Susan Crews-Hart

After two years of testing, photographing, and writing, the Grandma Maggie's Kitchen: The Crews Family Cookbook is 80% complete! Susan and her team have documented 187 of 220 recipes — from Maggie's famous Sunday pot roast to her legendary pralines.

The cookbook will be professionally printed in 100 copies and distributed at the 2026 Annual Reunion. Additional copies can be ordered after the reunion. Estimated retail price: $35. Family price: $20.

Still needed: If you have one of Grandma Maggie's recipes that isn't in the collection yet, please submit it to Susan before June 1st! Email: susan.hart@example.com

🏠 Homestead Restoration Update

Submitted by: James Crews

The restoration of Harold Crews's 1924 farmhouse is progressing beautifully. The new roof is complete, all electrical has been updated, and the original pine floors are being refinished to their original luster.

"Every time we uncover something original in that house, I feel a connection to Harold and Edna," James said. "We found handwritten measurements on a door frame in Harold's handwriting — we're keeping that one visible."

The homestead is on track to be ready for the 2026 reunion, which will serve as a kind of re-dedication ceremony for the property.

🎉 Reunion 2026 — Save the Date!

Submitted by: Reunion Committee

12th Annual Crews Family Reunion: August 21–23, 2026 at the Heard County Homestead. Theme: "Roots & Branches — Celebrating Our History."

New this year: documentary film screening (a short film about our family history produced by Michael), the first-ever Family Trivia Championship, and the official cookbook reveal. RSVP link will be sent in June. Mark your calendars!

⏰ RSVP Due: July 31

🌟 Family Spotlight: Susan's Art Show

Submitted by: Jennifer Crews-Moore

Our very own Susan Crews-Hart had her first solo gallery show at the Westside Gallery in Atlanta last February! Her collection, "Georgia in the Blood," featured 24 original paintings inspired by the family's Georgia landscapes and history.

"I never expected the response," Susan said. "Several pieces referenced the homestead, and family members who came to the opening were moved in ways I didn't anticipate." Four prints are available for family purchase — contact Susan!
