Stringer Family Genealogy

From Norman England to the American South. The Stringer name traces back to the craftsmen who made bowstrings — a vital trade in medieval warfare — and their descendants who helped settle the New World.

🏰 The Stringer Name — A Norman Legacy

The Stringer name comes from the ancient Norman culture established in Britain after the Conquest of 1066. It was derived from the German word streng, meaning "strong or courageous" — but more specifically became an occupational name for "one who made strings for bows."

"In war if a string break, the man is lost and is no man, and his weapon is gone... therefore, God send us good stringers both for war and peace."

The surname was first found in Nottinghamshire, where they held a family seat from early times as Lords of the manor of Eaton, conjecturally descended from Fulk, who held the lands from Roger de Bully at the time of the Domesday Book in 1086. The name grew to become "a common Yorkshire name."

Early records: Walter Stringere (Wiltshire, 1194) · Roger le Strenger (Yorkshire, 1293) · Samuel Stringer (Virginia, 1622) · John Stringer (Virginia, 1651)

The Direct Line — Alycia Stringer's Ancestry

From Nantwich, Cheshire, England to Alabama, generation by generation.

Generation 1 — England
1 John Stringer
b. c. 1590 · Nantwich, Cheshire, England
The earliest documented Stringer ancestor in this line. Born in Nantwich, Cheshire — a town famous for its salt production and medieval history in the north of England.
FamilySearch KFYS-1V4
Generation 2 — England
2 Gabriell Stringer
b. c. 1619, Nantwich, Cheshire – d. May 4, 1669 (Gloucestershire)
Also known as Gabriel, he was an innkeeper by trade. He married Isabell (Habbell) (b. 1628). His will was proved on May 4, 1669 — a rare surviving document that gives us a window into 17th-century English family life. He bequeathed his estate to his wife Habbell and his son Gabriell.
"In the name of God Amen I Gabriell Stringer of Hampnett in the County of Gloucester being very sick and weak in body but of good memory (praised be the Lord) doe make my last will and testament..."

Generation 4 — England to America
4 John Stringer, Jr.
Christened December 27, 1679, Cheshire – d. April 12, 1718 · Elizabeth City, Virginia
Also known as "John Stringloe." He crossed the Atlantic and settled in Elizabeth City, Virginia — one of the earliest American Stringers. He was a weaver, carpenter, cooper, and barrel maker. He married Margaret Mary Molyneux on July 20, 1699 in Cheshire before emigrating. He died young at ~39 in the Virginia colony.

Generation 5 — Colonial America
5 John Stringer III
b. 1714, Elizabeth City, Virginia – d. 1771 · Edgecombe County, North Carolina
Married Katherine Batchelder in 1739 in Edgecombe, North Carolina. The family moved south from Virginia into the Carolinas during the colonial period.
FamilySearch GQ5W-ZHT
Generation 6 — Revolutionary Era
6 Charles E. Stringer
b. January 5, 1748, Edgecombe County, NC – d. 1823–1831 · Lawrence County, Mississippi
Charles E. Stringer was an ordained Christian minister who "labored in some of the Southern States." Around 1809, he co-published a pamphlet with Elder Rainy in vindication of Christian doctrine. He won land in the 1806 Georgia Land Lottery and was on the first grand jury of the first court in Laurens County, GA in 1807. By 1818 he had sold all his land there and moved his family to Lawrence County, Mississippi, where he is buried in the Stringer Cemetery.

He married Sarah "Sally" Jones (1742–1799) in 1768 in Edgecombe County, NC. Together they had 15 children: Frances, Agnes, Cynthia, Josiah, Lawrence, John, Zachariah, Isaac, Leonard, Simon, Reddin, Gray, Arthur, Jacob, and Thomas.

FamilySearch KJ8D-GF4
Generation 7 — Deep South
7 John E. Stringer
b. 1772, North Carolina – d. October 1, 1855 · Lowndes County, Alabama
John E. married three times: first to Elizabeth Cawthon (b. 1776, d. 1850); then Sarah Brooks (b. 1791, d. 1868, m. February 5, 1851); and also Sarah Ann Jones (b. 1790, d. 1855). He settled in Lowndes County, Alabama, continuing the family's journey south and west.
FamilySearch GLZV-KDG
Generation 8 — Alabama
8 John Ashley Stringer
b. December 31, 1818 – d. December 13, 1895 · Spring Creek, Butler County, Alabama
Married Frances Susan Champion (b. 1810, d. 1851) on February 28, 1839 in Lowndes County, Alabama. In 1880, the family lived at Steiner's Store in Butler County. John Ashley helped establish the Stringer family's presence in the Alabama hill country.
