Herring Family Genealogy

From the lordships of medieval Scotland and the fishing villages of Dorset, England — across the Atlantic to the American South. The Herring line stretches back over 800 years. And it includes one of the most cherished family stories of all: Grandaddy Herring's journey to the Holy Land.

Robert "Bob" Herring
The Israel Trip of 1969

A Journey of Faith · A Life Well Lived · A Legacy That Endures

Passed Away
1969 · Tel Aviv, Israel
Heart attack at the airport

In 1969, Robert "Bob" Herring — "Grandaddy Herring" — embarked on what was surely the adventure of a lifetime: a journey to the Holy Land of Israel. He kept a journal of his travels, recording his experiences in a land of ancient stones, sacred sites, and living history. This journal, Herring Israel Trip 1969, is a treasured piece of family history — his own words, written as he walked where history was made.

"He traveled to see the Holy Land — and he wrote down everything he witnessed, so that his family would always know where he had been and what he had felt."

Bob and Lora Mae had built a large family together — at least six sons and five daughters — over more than four decades of marriage. He had lived a full, faithful life in Greenville, Butler County, Alabama. At the age of approximately 70, he made the journey across the world to walk the streets of Jerusalem, see the Sea of Galilee, and visit the places he had read about his entire life.

His Final Moments

The trip ended in tragedy. As Bob Herring made his way to the airport to board his flight home — ready to return to his family, to Greenville, to Lora Mae — he suffered a heart attack. He passed away there in Israel, in Tel Aviv, never making it home.

It is a story that carries both sorrow and a certain grace. He had seen the Holy Land. He had done what he set out to do. He had walked in places of deep meaning to him, and he had written about it so others could follow in his footsteps. His journal remains — a gift from Grandaddy to every generation that comes after.

Grandaddy Herring made it to the promised land — and he left his words behind so we could go there with him, page by page.
📖 The Journal: Robert "Bob" Herring's handwritten (or typed) journal of his 1969 Israel trip is preserved in the family archives as Herring_Israel_Trip_1969.pdf. This document is an irreplaceable piece of family history — his firsthand account of what he saw, felt, and experienced in the Holy Land. Future generations can read Grandaddy's own words and travel with him.

Lora Mae Boutwell Herring

Bob's wife, Lora Mae Boutwell (October 27, 1907 – October 29, 2004), outlived him by 35 years, reaching the remarkable age of 97. She was born in Georgiana, Alabama and married Bob on April 15, 1922 in De Funiak Springs, Florida. She was known for telling stories of washing clothes in the creek by hand. She died in Greenville, Alabama and is buried at Sunrise Memorial Park, Greenville, Butler County, Alabama.

Their children include: Vernon Stabler Herring (January 6, 1933 – December 3, 2014), who married Dorothy Mae Adams in 1955. Vernon is buried at Sunrise Cemetery in Greenville, alongside his mother.

The Herring Line — Back to England (and Scotland)

The Herring family traces back over 800 years — from noble Scottish lords to Dorset fisherfolk to Virginia tobacco farmers to Alabama families.

c. 1199–1259 — Scotland
🏰 The Herring Clan — Scotland
The Herring family has documented roots in Scotland from as early as 1199. They held lands and lordships in the Edinburgh and Midlothian regions.
Scotland
c. 1240 — Scotland
⚜ Sir John Herring of Edmonston
b. c. 1240 · Gilmerton, Edinburghshire · Buried: Abbey of Holyrood, Edinburgh
Lord of Gilmerton, Drum, and Goodtrees — a nobleman of considerable standing in medieval Scotland. Genealogists have identified him as a possible ancestor of President Benjamin Harrison, HRH King Charles, and Prime Minister Churchill (through different descendant lines). His daughter, Giles "Jill" Herring (b. 1265), married Sir Walter Somerville, connecting the Herrings to one of Scotland's great families.
Scottish Lord
c. 1270–1365 — England (Dorset)
🌊 The Dorset Herrings — Langton Herring & Chaldon Herring
The English branch settled in Dorset, where several villages — Langton Herring, Chaldon Herring, and Winterbourne Herringstone — bear the family name to this day. The main line in Dorset includes:

c. 1607 — Bristol, England → Virginia, America
⛵ John Herring — First American Ancestor
b. c. 1607, Bristol, England – d. c. 1672 · Isle of Wight County, Virginia
John Herring sailed from Bristol, England to Virginia — the Herring Book dates his arrival to around 1642; another source places it May 2, 1635 on the ship Alexander. He settled near the Blackwater River in the vicinity of Horse Swamp, northwest of present-day Windsor, Isle of Wight County. He was a tobacco farmer who also made tar. He married Marjorie (possibly Whitfield). His will was proved June 10, 1672.
"In the name of GOD Amen I being in good & perfect memory first I commit my body to the Earth & into the hands of Almighty GOD & Savior Jesus Christ. Give unto my Son Anthony Herring two steers of four years old... I give my son Anthony three barrels of tar... I give & bequeath all the rest of my whole Estate... unto my beloved wife Margorie Herring."
— John Herring, will proved June 10, 1672

The Boutwell Connection

Lora Mae Boutwell (Bob's wife) brought another remarkable lineage to the family — the Boutwells, who trace back to Scotland and the colonial Carolinas.

Boutwell Lineage (Lora Mae's Side)

The Boutwell line begins with the Hepburn/Bothwell family of Scotland — traced from Sir Patrick Hepburn (b. 1322, Prestonkirk, Scotland) through successive generations into Edinburgh — then emigrating to Virginia in the 1630s as William Bothwell (christened 1630, Canongate, Edinburgh → Elizabeth City, VA). The family name gradually became "Boutwell" in America.

William Bothwell (1630, Scotland → Virginia) → Adam Boutwell (1660, Edinburgh → Elizabeth City VA) → Samuel Boutwell Sr. (1682, Carolina → Georgetown SC) → Burtenhead Boutwell (1710 Virginia → South Carolina, Plantation Owner) → Lionel Delaney Boutwell (1733, Onslow NC → Barnwell SC) → William Delaney Boutwell (1760, Darlington SC → Pike County AL) → Deloney Burton "Lona" Boutwell Sr. (1795, Darlington SC → Lowndes AL) → Henry Burton Boutwell (1834 → Wilcox AL) [Served: Company M, 6th Alabama Inf., Confederate] → Herbert F. Boutwell (1866, Steep Creek, Lowndes AL → Fairhope, Baldwin AL) → Lora Mae Boutwell (1907, Georgiana AL → wife of Bob Herring)

Rosa Bell Boutwell Herring (1888–1970)

Sister of Lora Mae. Born November 8, 1888 in Garland, Alabama. Her first husband, Jesse Paul Herring (1882–1908), tragically died falling from railroad tracks while working on the L&N Railroad. She later married George Washington Herring on April 6, 1913 in Greenville. Together they had at least 8 children. She lived to 81.

Vernon Stabler Herring (1933–2014)

Son of Robert "Bob" Herring and Lora Mae Boutwell. Born January 6, 1933 in Greenville, Butler County, Alabama. Married Dorothy Mae Adams on September 9, 1955 in Etowah County, Alabama. Died December 3, 2014, at age 81. Buried at Sunrise Cemetery, Greenville.
