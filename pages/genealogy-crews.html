Generation 2
2 John Daniel Crews Sr. (Cruse)
1790, Georgia – 1880 · Crews Cemetery, Samson, Geneva County, Alabama
Born in Georgia, John Daniel made his way to Alabama where he established deep roots. He married Elizabeth Moye on July 15, 1830. He lived to the remarkable age of 90, and was buried in the Crews Cemetery in Samson — a cemetery that still bears the family name today. His sons included several who served as Confederate soldiers.

3 Isham David Crews
1815, Fitzgerald, Georgia – May 26, 1862 · Camp Randall, Dane County, Madison, Wisconsin
Isham David Crews enlisted in the Confederate Army and was captured by Union forces. He died in 1862 at Camp Randall — the infamous prisoner-of-war camp in Madison, Wisconsin, where hundreds of Confederate prisoners perished from disease and harsh conditions. He never returned home. He married Mary Ann Rouse (1823–1902) and had seven known children.

⚔️ Isham David Crews — A Prisoner of War

Isham David Crews was born in Fitzgerald, Georgia in 1815 and married Mary Ann Rouse. When the Civil War broke out, he enlisted in the Confederate Army. He was captured by Union forces and transferred to Camp Randall in Madison, Wisconsin — one of the North's most notorious prison camps.

Conditions at Camp Randall were brutal. In the spring of 1862, a deadly epidemic swept through the camp. Hundreds of Confederate prisoners died from disease, exposure, and inadequate food. Isham David Crews died there on May 26, 1862 — far from home, far from his wife Mary Ann, and far from his six children who would never see him again.

His sacrifice and story live on in every branch of the Crews family tree. His son, John Benjamin Crews, went on to live 96 years and raise a large family — ensuring the Crews name would continue.

Source: Find A Grave Memorial #7077647
