The Hidden Haven — Ilyth'Varan

Krynn Thal'zath was born beneath a canopy so dense it turned daylight into twilight. His home, Ilyth'Varan, was a hidden drow haven deep within a vast eastern rainforest — far removed from the cruelty most surface dwellers associated with his kind. Here, the drow had chosen a different path. They built their cities into colossal trees, wove bridges from living vines, and carved their history into stone temples scattered across the jungle floor.

They did not reject darkness. They balanced it.

To outsiders, Ilyth'Varan was a myth. To its people, it was proof that the drow could be more than what the world expected. Krynn believed that — mostly.

🏛️

A Different Kind of Curiosity

While others trained in survival, diplomacy, or defense of the haven, Krynn became obsessed with the ruins beyond it.

Because Ilyth'Varan wasn't the first civilization in that rainforest. Far older structures lay hidden beneath roots and soil — cyclopean stone temples, sealed vaults, and buried corridors no living drow had built. The elders taught caution: Respect the past. Do not disturb what sleeps.

Krynn heard something else entirely: Find out why it was buried in the first place.

🕸️

The Pact — A Chamber Beyond Time

His turning point came during a solo expedition — something he was explicitly forbidden from attempting. Deep within an overgrown ruin, Krynn uncovered a chamber untouched by time. No decay. No plant life. Just smooth black stone etched with patterns that seemed to shift at the edge of perception.

At its center was not an object — but an imprint of awareness. An intelligence bound into the structure itself. It did not introduce itself. It did not threaten him. It showed him things:

  • 🌆Cities swallowed by jungle and time
  • 🔄Civilizations that rose, uncovered truths, and vanished
  • 🌀A pattern repeating across ages — over and over, endlessly

It offered him power — but framed it as purpose:

"Continue what others abandoned."

Krynn accepted. Not for power. For answers.

Zhaun'ylith
The Web Beyond Worlds

Consulting with the Elders after his return, Krynn came to believe he had encountered Zhaun'ylith — known in ancient lore as The Web Beyond Worlds. A being woven through the hidden places of the earth, long buried and largely forgotten. The Elders knew only fragmentary tales: an intelligence that persisted in the spaces between civilizations, observing patterns that spanned millennia. Its nature — benevolent, neutral, or something far stranger — remains one of the central mysteries Krynn is driven to unravel.

⚡

Tension with Home

When Krynn returned, something about him had changed. Not visibly — but in the way he spoke about the ruins. He no longer saw them as dangerous relics to respect. He saw them as unfinished stories. That unsettled people.

Ilyth'Varan valued harmony — between nature, history, and restraint. Krynn began to push against that restraint:

  • 🔓He went deeper than others would
  • 📦Opened sealed chambers that had been sealed for good reason
  • 🗿Brought back artifacts some believed should remain buried

To him, this was progress. To others, it felt like risk… or even betrayal. Eventually, the tension reached a quiet conclusion. Krynn was encouraged to leave — not as punishment, but as necessity.

"If you must chase these truths… do it somewhere their consequences won't fall on us."
🎓

The University — Where He Thrived

Krynn traveled west and eventually found his way into a prestigious university known for archaeology and arcane study. Here, he thrived. His field instincts were exceptional. His knowledge of ruins surpassed most others. His warlock abilities gave him an edge he never fully explained.

Krynn recently graduated, his thesis on "Submerged Civilizations and the Persistence of Living Architecture" drawing significant recognition from the faculty. During the semester he prepared his thesis, Krynn left the University to find solitude and quiet to write and refine it. While he was away, some of his friends had the opportunity to seek out a relic for the professor.

Of course, they took the opportunity — and they would have loved to have Krynn with them. But he could not be found in time.

Fate? Or chance?
🗺️

Now — The Adventure Begins

Now that he has returned and is graduated, Krynn cannot wait to join the next expedition. The ruins that consumed his youth, the pact that gave him purpose, the missing pieces his friends found without him — all of it pulls him forward.

Somewhere out there, the pattern Zhaun'ylith showed him is still unfolding. Civilizations rise, discover something they shouldn't, and vanish. Krynn intends to be the first one to break that cycle.

Or at least to understand it before it swallows him too.
