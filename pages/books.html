Family Book List

Recommended reads, book club picks, and family favorites — for all ages

📖 Book Club — Currently Reading

📗
Killers of the Flower Moon
David Grann · 2017 · Non-Fiction / History
★★★★★ Book Club Pick Reading Now
The chilling true story of the Osage murders in 1920s Oklahoma and the birth of the FBI. A remarkable piece of American history that reads like a thriller.
💬 "Patricia: 'I could not put this down. Essential American history.'"

🌟 Crews Family Classics

📙
To Kill a Mockingbird
Harper Lee · 1960 · Fiction / Coming-of-Age
★★★★★Family Classic
A masterpiece of American literature set in the Deep South. Robert Crews said this book "captures the Georgia I grew up in." A rite of passage read for every Crews teenager.
💬 "Robert Crews called this 'the most important book I ever read.'"
📘
Gone with the Wind
Margaret Mitchell · 1936 · Historical Fiction
★★★★★Family Classic
A sprawling epic of the Civil War-era South. Edna Crews owned a first edition copy, now preserved in the family archive. A beloved read across all generations of the family.
💬 "Edna's first edition copy is kept in the family archive — handle with care!"
📕
The Grapes of Wrath
John Steinbeck · 1939 · Fiction / Drama
★★★★★Family Classic
Harold Crews reportedly read this aloud to his children during the Great Depression, saying the Joad family reminded him of neighbors. A powerful story of resilience that resonates with our family's history.

✅ Recently Read — Family Recommendations

📕
The Lincoln Highway
Amor Towles · 2021 · Fiction / Adventure
★★★★½Highly Rec'd
An adventure story following an 18-year-old and his younger brother on an unexpected journey across America. A page-turner with humor and heart.
💬 "Michael: 'Read it on the beach — perfect summer book.'"
📘
Hillbilly Elegy
J.D. Vance · 2016 · Memoir / Non-Fiction
★★★★
A memoir about growing up in Appalachian Ohio that sparked lively family discussions about rural American culture and our own family roots in the Georgia countryside.
💬 "David: 'Really made us think about where we came from.'"
📗
Hidden Valley Road
Robert Kolker · 2020 · Non-Fiction / Family
★★★★½
The extraordinary story of a mid-century American family with twelve children, six of whom were diagnosed with schizophrenia. A deeply moving look at family bonds and medical history.
💬 "Jennifer: 'One of the most powerful family stories I've ever read.'"
📙
The Warmth of Other Suns
Isabel Wilkerson · 2010 · History / Non-Fiction
★★★★★Must-Read
The epic story of the Great Migration through three individuals' journeys. Provides essential context for understanding the American South and the era our family history spans.
