Chuck's Reading List

Fantasy epics, sci-fi classics, thrillers, and everything in between

🧙 Epic Fantasy

🌑
The Mistborn Original Trilogy
Brandon Sanderson · 2006–2008 · Epic Fantasy
3 Books★★★★★
A world where ash falls from the sky and the prophesied hero failed — a thousand years ago. What followed was the Lord Ruler's empire. What follows now is a thief crew's heist to steal an entire empire. Sanderson's magic system (Allomancy) is the most inventive in modern fantasy. The ending of Book 3 is legendary.
1. The Final Empire (2006) 2. The Well of Ascension (2007) 3. The Hero of Ages (2008)
💍
The Lord of the Rings Trilogy
J.R.R. Tolkien · 1954–1955 · High Fantasy
3 Books★★★★★
The foundation of modern fantasy. Tolkien invented an entire world — languages, histories, mythologies — before he wrote a single page of story. The Fellowship, the journey to Mordor, the siege of Minas Tirith. There is no other work like it and there never will be.
1. The Fellowship of the Ring (1954) 2. The Two Towers (1954) 3. The Return of the King (1955)
🗺️
The Belgariad
David Eddings · 1982–1984 · Epic Fantasy
5 Books★★★★★
A farm boy named Garion discovers he is the center of a prophecy stretching across millennia — and that his Aunt Pol is the most powerful sorceress alive. An essential quest fantasy with unforgettable characters and Eddings' warmly witty dialogue. The world-building is rich and the ensemble cast is one of fantasy's finest.
1. Pawn of Prophecy (1982) 2. Queen of Sorcery (1982) 3. Magician's Gambit (1983) 4. Castle of Wizardry (1984) 5. Enchanters' End Game (1984)
📙
Dune
Frank Herbert · 1965 · Science Fantasy / Epic
★★★★★Essential
The greatest science fiction novel ever written — and arguable the greatest novel, period. A desert planet, a messianic hero, political intrigue spanning interstellar empires, and a meditation on religion, ecology, and power that still feels urgent today. The Villeneuve films finally did it justice. Read the book first.
📕
Unfinished Tales of Númenor and Middle-earth
J.R.R. Tolkien (ed. Christopher Tolkien) · 1980 · Fantasy / Lore
★★★★½For LOTR Fans
The essential companion to Lord of the Rings — stories and essays that expand the history of Middle-earth. Covers the full tale of Tuor, Galadriel and Celeborn, the Istari, the Palantíri, and much more. Tolkien's world-building runs deeper than any single book could hold.
📘
Shōgun
James Clavell · 1975 · Historical Fiction
★★★★★Epic Historical
An English navigator is shipwrecked in feudal Japan in 1600 — a world of warring warlords, rigid honor codes, and violent beauty. A masterwork of historical fiction that immerses you so completely in feudal Japan you feel you have lived there. One of the greatest novels of the 20th century. The 2024 FX series is equally exceptional.

🚀 Science Fiction

🤖
The Murderbot Diaries
Martha Wells · 2017–2023 · Sci-Fi / Hugo Award Winner
7 Books★★★★★Hugo Winner
A self-hacking security robot — it calls itself Murderbot — just wants to watch its TV serials and be left alone. Unfortunately, the humans it's supposed to guard keep getting into trouble. Wildly funny, surprisingly moving, and a genuine exploration of autonomy and identity. The first four novellas are compulsively readable. Network Effect won the Hugo for Best Novel.
1. All Systems Red (2017) 2. Artificial Condition (2018) 3. Rogue Protocol (2018) 4. Exit Strategy (2018) 5. Network Effect (2020) — Novel 6. Fugitive Telemetry (2021) 7. System Collapse (2023)
📗
To Your Scattered Bodies Go
Philip José Farmer · 1971 · Science Fiction · Riverworld #1
★★★★★Hugo Winner
Every human who ever lived wakes up on the banks of an infinite river on an alien world — no one knows why. Richard Francis Burton (the explorer) wakes up and immediately begins trying to find out. A staggering premise, executed brilliantly. One of the great Hugo Award-winning novels and an endlessly fascinating thought experiment about humanity itself.
📗
A Psalm for the Wild-Built
Becky Chambers · 2021 · Sci-Fi / Solarpunk · Monk & Robot #1
★★★★★Hugo Winner
A tea monk travels beyond the edges of civilization and encounters a robot — the first robot to speak to a human in generations. They sit together and talk about what people need. A quiet, deeply humane novella that asks "what do you want?" and doesn't pretend the answer is easy. A perfect antidote to grimdark. Chambers at her best.
📘
Recursion
Blake Crouch · 2019 · Thriller / Sci-Fi
★★★★★Mind-Bending
A neuroscientist develops technology that lets people revisit their memories — but each use rewrites reality for everyone else. A time-loop thriller that escalates with mounting dread and genuine emotional stakes. Crouch's best book. Impossible to put down, and the climax is earned in ways that will wreck you.

🔫 Thrillers & Action

🥊
Jack Reacher Series
Lee Child / Andrew Child · 1997–Present · Action Thriller
29 Books★★★★★
A drifter ex-military cop with no fixed address, no phone, and no patience for injustice. Reacher wanders into small towns and corrupt systems alike, and leaves only when justice has been served — usually in a physically catastrophic way. The purest action-thriller series in print. Start with Killing Floor or One Shot.
Killing Floor (1997) Die Trying (1998) Tripwire (1999) Running Blind (2000) Echo Burning (2001) Without Fail (2002) Persuader (2003) The Enemy (2004) One Shot (2005) The Hard Way (2006) Bad Luck and Trouble (2007) Nothing to Lose (2008) Gone Tomorrow (2009) 61 Hours (2010) Worth Dying For (2010) The Affair (2011) A Wanted Man (2012) Never Go Back (2013) Personal (2014) Make Me (2015) Night School (2016) The Midnight Line (2017) Past Tense (2018) Blue Moon (2019) The Sentinel (2020) Better Off Dead (2021) No Plan B (2022) The Secret (2023) In Too Deep (2024)
🎣
Dave Robicheaux Series
James Lee Burke · 1987–2022 · Crime / Literary Thriller
23 Books★★★★★Edgar Award Winner
Cajun detective Dave Robicheaux — recovering alcoholic, Vietnam vet, father, and the most poetic voice in American crime fiction — navigates the bayous of New Iberia, Louisiana. Burke writes sentences that belong in literature anthologies. The violence is real, the landscape is alive, and Robicheaux carries the weight of the world on his shoulders and keeps walking anyway.
The Neon Rain (1987) Heaven's Prisoners (1988) Black Cherry Blues (1989) ★ Edgar A Morning for Flamingoes (1990) A Stained White Radiance (1992) In the Electric Mist with Confederate Dead (1993) Dixie City Jam (1994) Burning Angel (1995) Sunset Limited (1998) Purple Cane Road (2000) Jolie Blon's Bounce (2002) Last Car to Elysian Fields (2003) Crusader's Cross (2005) The Tin Roof Blowdown (2007) Swan Peak (2008) The Glass Rainbow (2010) Creole Belle (2012) Light of the World (2013) Robicheaux (2018) The New Iberia Blues (2019) A Private Cathedral (2020) Every Cloak Rolled in Blood (2022)
📙
The Old Man
Thomas Perry · 2017 · Thriller
★★★★★High-Octane
A retired CIA operative living quietly under a false identity is suddenly hunted by men who should never have been able to find him. What follows is a masterclass in thriller writing — a man who has lived too long in the dark, using every skill he has just to survive another day. The FX series with Jeff Bridges is equally excellent. A lean, propulsive, unforgettable read.
📘
The Sword of God
Mark Dawson · 2014 · Thriller · John Milton #4
★★★★½Action Thriller
John Milton — one of Britain's most lethal government assassins, now gone off the grid — finds himself in the remote mountains of Michigan, in the middle of a militia movement preparing for holy war. Dawson writes at a breakneck pace. The Milton series is a class act, and this entry is among the best.
📕
Run
Blake Crouch · 2011 · Thriller
★★★★½Relentless
A mass-casualty event triggers a new kind of America, where survival means keeping moving. A family flees for their lives across a collapsing country. Raw, kinetic, and brutally tense. Crouch at his most unfiltered — before the science-fiction twists of his later work, this is pure chase thriller, and it never stops.
📘
Tom Clancy: Line of Demarcation
Tom Clancy · Military / Political Thriller
★★★★Military Thriller
The latest in the Jack Ryan universe — geopolitical brinkmanship, intelligence operations, and the machinery of American power under pressure. Clancy's universe remains the gold standard for military-political thrillers, with a depth of technical and strategic detail that no one else matches.

🕯️ Horror & Dark Fiction

📕
The Fisherman
John Langan · 2016 · Horror / Weird Fiction
★★★★★Cosmic Horror
Two grieving men — each widowed — bond over fishing along the Catskill streams of New York. When they hear of a legendary spot called Dutchman's Creek, the story that follows is a slow, suffocating descent into myth and cosmic dread. A campfire horror story about grief, loss, and the terrible things we might do to bring someone back. The best horror novel of the past decade. Langan writes like Poe with the architecture of a master storyteller.

📖 Literary Fiction

📕
The Darkest Child
Delores Phillips · 2004 · Literary Fiction
★★★★★Powerful
Set in 1950s rural Georgia, this devastating novel follows a Black family under the complete psychological domination of their mother, Rozelle — a woman of terrifying beauty and cruelty. Told through the eyes of her youngest daughter, Tangy Mae, it's a story about survival, love, and what it means to hold yourself together when everything is designed to break you. One of the most powerful and underread American novels ever written.
📗
Someone You Can Build a Nest In
John Wiswell · 2024 · Fantasy / Literary Fiction
★★★★★2024 Nebula Winner
A shape-shifting monster who simply wants to be left alone falls in love with the monster hunter sent to kill her. Tender, funny, and deeply strange — Wiswell writes with a warmth that makes you root for a creature designed to frighten. A love story for people who feel like they don't quite belong in the world. The 2024 Nebula Award winner for Best Novel.
📙
The Silent Patient
Alex Michaelides · 2019 · Psychological Thriller
★★★★★Bestseller
A famous painter shoots her husband five times in the face — then never speaks another word. A forensic psychotherapist becomes obsessed with uncovering the truth. The twist is one of the best in modern thriller history. You'll read it in one sitting and immediately want to read it again knowing what you know.
